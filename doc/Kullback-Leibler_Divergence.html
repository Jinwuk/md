Kullback-Leibler Divergence

KL Divergence를 최소화 하는 문제는 결국 Maximum Likelihood를 최대화 하는 문제로 귀결됨을 보인다. (뭐 당연하지만..)
Assume that two probability distribution p and q.

Definition KL Divergence

Discrete Version

KL(p||q)≜∑k=1Kpklogpkqk=∑k=1Kpklogpk−∑k=1Kpklogqk=−H(p)+H(p,q)

where H(p) is the entropy of p such that

H(X)≜−∑k=1Kp(X=k)log2p(X=k)

and H(p,q) is called the cross entropy such that

H(p,q)≜−∑kpklogqk

Continuous Version

KL(p||q)≜∫∞∞p(x)logp(x)q(x)dx

If p(x),q(X) are probablity measure over a set X and p(x) is absolutely continuous with repect to q(x) then

KL(p||q)=∫XlogdPdQdP(1)

Correspondence to Radon-Nykodym Derivation

Remind the equation (1), where dPdQ is the Radon=Nikodym derivative of P with Q, and the equation (1) can be rewritten as

KL=∫Xlog(dPdQ)dPdQdQ

which we recognize the entropy of P relative to Q.

Assume that there exist the value of Radon-Nykodym Derivation such that μ=dPdQ then

KL=∫Xlogμ⋅μdQ=∫XμlogμdQ=−H(μ)

Correspondence to Girsanov Theorem

Assume that the following SDE

dWt=dBt+Htdt

where Bt is the brownian motion measured by Q and Wt is the brownian motion measured by P. Thus,
the Radon-Nykodym Derivative is evaluated by the Girsanov Theorem

dPdQ=exp(−∫t0HsdBs−12∫t0H2sds)=Λ

Then

KL=∫X(−∫t0HsdBs−12∫t0H2sds)logΛdQ

If we want to minimize the KL Divergence, it maximize the Radon-Nykodym derivative.
Therefore, it is a same problem of maximum likelihood problem.

이렇게 되면 방정식 (1)에 따라 Likelihood 함수는 다음과 같다.

Λ(X)T=dPdQ=exp(∫T0−αXtσ2dXt−12∫T0α2X2tσ2dt)

이때 exp 내부를 미분하여 최대값이 나오는 α를 구하면
